The Central Innovation District Hub lets the (future) residents, entrepreneurs, organisations, and developers of this major development work together to design this new district.

This district will redefine the skyline of the city, housing 35,000 new citizens in over 20,000 new houses. As part of a student project, we were tasked with boosting participation with the various stakeholders in the district.

Our goal was to create a space that could engage the youth and skilled migrants because these were the audiences that the municipality had difficulties reaching.

Research

In our research, we focused on understanding the youth's perception of the CID, their communication preferences regarding government information, and their motivation for visiting public information centers.

Our goal was to create a space that could engage the youth and skilled migrants because these were the audiences that the municipality had difficulties reaching.

As research methods, we used surveys, interviews, stakeholder maps, and observations, and triangulated all the qualitative data with quantitative data provided by the municipality.

Final concept

The final concept is a a dynamic co-creation space hosting regular events to engage diverse subcultures and gather valuable feedback for the CID area's future development.

The space is modular, and enables the community to use it according to their needs. It can be used both as a regular information space, but also as a space that hosts community events.
